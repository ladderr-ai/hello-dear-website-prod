Everything you need to know about Hello Dear

What is Hello Dear?

Hello Dear is an AI phone companion for seniors that calls regularly to reduce loneliness and uplift spirits through warm conversation. It helps keep them on their medication schedule, maintains daily routines, and gently encourages healthy habits like taking a short walk, doing light exercises, staying hydrated, or getting some fresh air—all through their regular phone with no apps to learn.

Why did you create Hello Dear? (the full story)

My mum was put on new medication and kept forgetting to take it. I started calling her at medication times and it helped, but I couldn't always be there—meetings, work travel, life gets in the way. As a software engineer, I built a simple automated calling system that would ring her phone at the right moments with a gentle reminder. That made a real difference.

But I realized a reminder alone felt cold and transactional. I thought—what if it could have a proper conversation with her? So I spent evenings and weekends building a system using AI that could have natural, caring chats. I programmed it to ask about her day, her plants, family stories, and talk to her. I taught it to gently suggest activities—have a glass of water, do a short stretch, step outside if it's nice. The technical challenge was getting the AI to feel warm and genuinely caring, not robotic.

The first time I heard her excitement telling me what she'd talked about on the call—a tip about when to add dried lemon to Ghormeh Sabzi for the best flavor, a childhood memory it had brought back—I realized something powerful. She'd started calling it "her friend on the phone." I hadn't heard her this animated after an ordinary call in years. She wasn't just taking her medication anymore; she had something to look forward to each day.

I realized you don't have to force new tech on seniors to give them the benefits of AI. Keep the familiar tool—their regular phone—and add a warm, intelligent companion on the other end. I built Hello Dear for my mum first. Now I'd like to offer it to your family too.

Is Hello Dear right for my family?

Hello Dear is ideal if your loved one prefers phone calls to apps and screens, would enjoy regular conversation with a kind voice, and could benefit from daily check-ins. It's especially helpful if you've noticed loneliness creeping in, routines slipping, or if you worry about them between your own visits. If they sometimes forget medications, skip meals, or spend too much time alone, a dependable call at the same time each day can make a meaningful difference.

How does Hello Dear actually work, step by step?

You sign up with your email. Because we care about reliability, we onboard people in small beta batches. After sign-up you can speak with the same kind of AI your loved one will talk to, so you can see how it feels. It asks a few questions about your needs and helps us prioritise your place in the next batch. You can also book a short call with me if you prefer a human conversation.

When it's your turn, you choose call times, set quiet hours, decide whether you want missed-call texts and simple summaries, and you confirm consent. Then we start the calls.

How do I sign up while you're in beta?

Add your email on the site. You'll be able to try the agent and, if you want, book a quick chat with me. We'll confirm your spot and bring you in with the next batch. We grow carefully so the service stays steady from day one.

What happens during a typical call?

A warm hello. A simple conversation about their day, hobbies, family memories, or a light brain game if they fancy it. Gentle encouragement for small healthy habits. If they don't feel like talking, the AI keeps it brief and tries again another time.

How long can each call last?

Up to 30 minutes. That keeps the conversation friendly and unhurried without being tiring.

Can I try it first to see if it helps?

Yes. There's a 7-day trial. Pick one clear goal—fewer missed tablets, a brighter mood, a short daily walk. Keep one call at the same time each day. Notice what changes between your own check-ins with them, and then ask your loved one after a week if they want to keep it.

Do you offer medication reminders?

We're running a small pilot of gentle, one-line medication nudges with a handful of customers. It's limited while we make sure it's accurate and genuinely helpful. These nudges are not medical advice. Please keep an independent backup because any technology can occasionally fail or be unavailable due to outages. A backup could be pharmacy packs, a pill organiser, a phone or watch alarm, or a family check-in. If you'd like early access, mention it when you sign up and we'll try to prioritise you.

Is Hello Dear an emergency or medical service?

No. Hello Dear is non-clinical and not an emergency line. For emergencies, call 999. For non-urgent medical advice, use NHS 111. We don't diagnose, monitor conditions, or replace clinical care—we offer friendly conversation and routine support.

What happens if my loved one doesn't pick up?

We retry later. If you switch on alerts, we send a text to a nominated relative after repeated no-answers so someone can check in. We always respect the quiet hours you set.

Can my loved one call Hello Dear themselves?

Yes, that feature is on the way. We're rolling out Call Me Anytime, which lets your loved one ring Hello Dear between scheduled check-ins for a quick chat. We'll enable it for beta customers as we scale each batch.

Do I need the senior's consent before I register them?

Yes. Please explain Hello Dear in plain language and get their clear "yes." If they don't have capacity to consent, you must have lawful authority, for example a Lasting Power of Attorney or a deputyship, and act in their best interests.

How do you handle privacy and security?

We collect the minimum we need to run the service. Calls and data travel over encrypted connections (TLS). We use trusted providers for hosting, telephony and AI, and some processing may happen outside the UK with safeguards. You can ask us to delete recordings and transcripts; we keep only the billing records we're legally required to keep. Full details are in our Privacy Policy.

Will AI ever say the wrong thing or go off topic?

Sometimes it will. AI can be unpredictable and occasionally say something a bit off. We tune it carefully and keep improving, but treat anything it says as unverified and never rely on it for medical, legal, financial, or safeguarding decisions.

Can software or phone networks fail now and then?

They can. We aim for steady service, but any system can have hiccups or outages. If you spot an issue, tell us and we'll investigate quickly. For anything critical, keep your own backups and checks in place.

How is this different from panic buttons or weekly befriending calls?

Panic buttons are for emergencies you trigger, and many befriending schemes are weekly. Hello Dear is daily, proactive, and uses the phone they already have, so there's nothing new to learn and the rhythm becomes part of everyday life.

How do I know if Hello Dear is really helping?

Give the trial a simple structure: one clear goal, one steady time, one week. Notice how they seem between your own check-ins with them. Are missed tablets fewer? Is mood a little brighter? Are short walks or glasses of water happening more often? Most importantly, ask your loved one if they enjoyed it.
