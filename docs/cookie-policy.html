Cookie Policy

Last Updated: 11 October 2025

This Cookie Policy explains how Hello Dear (operated by a sole trader in England) uses cookies, pixels, SDKs, local storage, advertising identifiers, and similar technologies (collectively, "Cookies") across our websites, dashboard, telephony interfaces, and communications (the "Services"). It supplements our Privacy Policy and Terms of Use.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small data files or code snippets saved to your device or browser. Similar technologies include local storage, mobile SDKs, pixels in emails, and advertising IDs. Cookies may be set by us (first-party) or by third parties. Some expire at the end of a session, others persist until removed.

2. Why We Use Cookies

We use Cookies to support the Services, including:

Strictly Necessary

Essential for authentication, security, call routing, load balancing, and storing your cookie choices. These are always active because the Services will not work without them.

Functional

Remember settings such as voice preferences, localisation, or interface customisations.

Performance & Analytics

Help us understand usage, troubleshoot issues, measure campaign effectiveness, and improve the Services.

Advertising & Measurement

Support marketing, retargeting, and attribution across platforms. These may track your activity on other sites.

Non-essential Cookies (functional, analytics, advertising) require your consent under PECR/ePrivacy rules. You can withdraw consent at any time.

4. Managing Your Preferences

  • Giving Consent: On first visit we display a consent banner where you can accept, reject, or customise non-essential Cookies. Non-essential cookies (analytics, advertising, functional) are ONLY loaded after you provide explicit consent via the banner. Strictly necessary cookies load immediately to enable core functionality (authentication, security, call routing).
  • Withdrawing Consent: You can withdraw your cookie consent at any time using any of these methods: (1) Click the "Cookie Settings" link in our website footer or dashboard to reopen the consent banner and change your preferences; (2) Email privacy@hellodear.ai with the subject line "Withdraw Cookie Consent" and we will manually disable non-essential cookies for your account; or (3) Clear your browser cookies (though this will also delete strictly necessary cookies and may impair functionality). Withdrawing consent is as easy as giving it.
  • We honour Global Privacy Control (GPC) and similar opt-out signals for advertising Cookies where technically feasible.
  • Strictly Necessary Cookies: Cookies required for authentication, security, call routing, and payment processing cannot be disabled within the Services. Blocking them via your browser may break core functionality (including sign-in and calls) and we are not responsible for resulting issues.
  • Your preferences are device- and browser-specific. Deleting Cookies or using private browsing resets your choices.

Important: Until we deploy our consent management platform, only strictly necessary cookies are active. When we introduce analytics and advertising cookies, we will implement a compliant consent banner before loading any non-essential cookies.

5. Browser and Device Controls

  • You can block or delete Cookies using your browser settings. Refer to your browser’s help documentation.
  • To opt out of Google Analytics everywhere, install the browser add-on at tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout.
  • For interest-based advertising choices visit optout.networkadvertising.org, optout.aboutads.info, or youronlinechoices.eu.
  • Mobile operating systems let you reset or limit ad identifiers in device privacy settings.

We cannot guarantee how third-party tools recognise or honour opt-outs. Managing Cookies is your responsibility.

6. Email Pixels and Telephony Analytics

Marketing emails may include tracking pixels to measure opens and clicks. Telephony providers (such as LiveKit, telecom carriers) may use session identifiers, cookies, or device fingerprints to route calls, detect spam, prevent fraud, or monitor call quality. These telephony cookies and identifiers are strictly necessary for call deliverability, security, and fraud detection.

If you prefer not to be tracked in emails, unsubscribe from marketing communications or disable image loading in your email client. Telephony session cookies cannot be disabled as they are essential for the Services to function.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy to reflect new technologies, vendors, or legal requirements. Revisions take effect when posted. Significant changes may be highlighted via the consent banner or email. Continued use of the Services after changes means you accept the updated Policy.
