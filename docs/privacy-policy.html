Privacy Policy

Last Updated: 11 October 2025

This Privacy Policy explains how the Hello Dear sole trader established in England ("Hello Dear", "we", "us") processes personal data when you access our AI phone companion, websites, dashboards, or related services (collectively, the "Services"). We aim to comply with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR), the Data Protection Act 2018, and, when applicable, the EU GDPR and the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR). This Policy should be read with our Terms of Use and Cookie Policy.

Hello Dear operates as an unincorporated sole trader; no limited company has been formed yet. Contact us at privacy@hellodear.ai regarding this Policy or data rights.

1. Summary

  • Controller: We act as data controller for personal data processed through the Services.
  • Third-Party Processing: The Services run on third-party infrastructure and AI models (including Amazon Web Services for hosting and storage, OpenAI, Anthropic, ElevenLabs, LiveKit, telecom carriers, analytics providers like Google and Meta, and similar vendors). These providers process data on our behalf and may store it outside the UK.
  • AI Unreliability: The Services WILL FREQUENTLY generate incorrect, offensive, or misleading content. We do not independently verify anything the AI systems say and we are not responsible for any harm caused by relying on outputs. This is NOT an emergency service and cannot dial 999 or detect medical emergencies.
  • Your Legal Authority: You must have lawful authority before supplying information about other people. If the person lacks mental capacity, you must hold a Lasting Power of Attorney, Court of Protection deputyship, or other lawful basis under the Mental Capacity Act 2005. If you cannot demonstrate a lawful basis, do not submit their data.
  • Security Limitations: We cannot guarantee security. We rely on supplier safeguards (primarily AWS managed services) and operational practices appropriate to the scale of our operations, but all storage and transmissions carry risk. Use the Services only if you accept this risk.
  • Call Recording: Calls may be recorded, transcribed, and analyzed for quality, safety, and service improvement purposes. You must obtain consent from all parties before enabling recording, as required by the Regulation of Investigatory Powers Act 2000.

3. Personal Data We Collect

Depending on how you use the Services we may process:

Account & Contact Data

  • Name, email address, phone number, role (for example family member or caregiver), relationship to Participants.
  • Account configuration details, support tickets, consent records.
  • Basic log information (IP address, timestamps) captured by hosting providers.

Participant & Profile Data

  • Details you provide about the person receiving calls (name, preferred topics, routines, medication names or reminders you enter).
  • Relationship notes or escalation instructions that you submit.

Communications & Audio

  • Phone numbers dialled, call duration, timestamps, completion status.
  • Audio recordings, transcriptions, summaries, and AI-generated responses when you enable those features.
  • SMS or chat content routed through the Services.

Technical & Usage Data

  • Device/browser information supplied by your device, session cookies, analytics identifiers.
  • Diagnostic and telemetry data captured by AWS or third-party SDKs.

Billing & Commercial Data

  • Subscription selections, invoices, payment confirmations (processed by Stripe or other payment providers).
  • Records needed for fraud prevention, accounting, and tax compliance.

Marketing & Cookies

  • Cookie identifiers, advertising identifiers, referral information, and marketing preferences where you opt in.
  • Email engagement metrics (opens, clicks) for newsletters or announcements.

Special Category Data: We do not deliberately require special category data (health information, biometric data, etc.), but the nature of the Services means you may choose to submit it. If you choose to submit special category data you are responsible for ensuring you have a lawful basis (explicit consent under UK GDPR Article 9) and the submission is strictly necessary.

Medication Information: If you enter medication names, dosages, schedules, or health conditions, you are solely responsible for accuracy. We do not review, verify, or validate medication data against medical records, prescriptions, or formularies. AI may misinterpret, misstate, or omit medication information. Do not rely on the Services for critical medication management.

Biometric Voice Data: Voice recordings may constitute biometric data under UK GDPR Article 9 if used for identification purposes. We do not currently use voice recordings for biometric identification, but our speech synthesis providers (ElevenLabs) may analyze voice characteristics to generate natural-sounding responses. By enabling voice features you consent to this processing where required by law.

5. Lawful Bases for Processing

We use the following lawful bases under UK GDPR and EU GDPR:

  • Performance of a contract – to create accounts, place calls, generate transcripts, and provide support.
  • Legitimate interests – to secure, maintain, and improve the Services; to investigate abuse; to conduct analytics; to send essential communications; and to promote our Services where the law allows. We balance these interests against your rights.
  • Consent – for non-essential cookies, marketing emails or SMS, optional recording where required, and for processing any special category data you choose to submit.
  • Explicit consent (Article 9) – Recording of calls containing health information, medication details, or other special category data requires EXPLICIT CONSENT under UK GDPR Article 9. By enabling call recording features you confirm you have obtained and documented such consent in writing before the first call. Consent for call recording under RIPA 2000 and PECR must also be obtained from all parties to the call before recording begins.
  • Legal obligations – to comply with tax, accounting, regulatory, or law-enforcement requirements.
  • Vital interests – only in rare situations where we reasonably believe it is necessary to protect someone's life or physical safety (for example, if we become aware of an immediate threat of serious harm).

9. Retention

  • Account records and billing data: Kept while your account is active and for up to six (6) years afterwards to meet legal, tax, and accounting obligations.
  • Call recordings and transcripts: Typically retained for up to one hundred eighty (180) days, unless you request earlier deletion or longer retention (subject to technical feasibility). Deleted data may persist in backups for up to ninety (90) days.
  • Analytics, telemetry, and cookie data: Retained for up to thirteen (13) months.
  • Marketing preferences: Stored until you opt out or close your account.
  • Data of deceased Participants: If a Participant passes away, we will delete their personal data upon written notice from you or their estate representative, subject to our legal obligations (for example, billing records, regulatory compliance). We may retain anonymised or aggregated data. Data of deceased persons may be retained longer where required for legal, regulatory, tax, or billing purposes.

We may anonymise or aggregate data for longer periods. We are not obligated to retain your information after the applicable retention window. Export anything you need before closing your account. Once deleted, data cannot be recovered.

You accept these inherent risks and are responsible for your own device security, account credentials, and for monitoring your account for suspicious activity. If you suspect unauthorized access or a data breach, contact us immediately at security@hellodear.ai.
